Boringer

I don't think anyone can argue that grammar isn't boring.

It's a set of rules that tell you how you can and cannot talk. Wouldn't we be much better off if we could just speak as we wanted to?

The only thing I find remotely interesting about grammar is finding typos and missing apostrophes in books and textbooks that are supposed to be all accurate. I remember, in second grade, being elated to find that the top of a math worksheet read "substraction." I know that's not necessarily grammar, but I like finding other people's flaws.

In my opinion, part of why most people (including me) find grammar boring is because of its bad reputation. When somebody says grammar, most people think of old teachers with dentures and cobweb hairdos tapping of thick, dusty textbooks with their canes.

I you still don't find that my ranting makes you feel better sbout learning grammar, then The Oatmeal is a good website to look at. The Oatmeal uses ridiculous sentences and fun drawings to point out the rights and wrongs in grammar.

Boringest

Waiting rooms are boring, aren't they?

Waiting rooms should not even exist.

Waiting should not exist.

I know the point of putting boring magazines and lame, unsanitary toys in a waiting room is to make it less boring for those unlucky individuals who have to wait for their doctor/dentist/whatever appointment. There is one TV hanging on the wall, and it's usually showing something like Mickey Mouse Clubhouse. Either that, or weight loss commercials. That is, if you are lucky. Some waiting rooms have an old TV that has, like, five channels.

The reading material is even worse. There are car magazines from, like, 2005, and beauty magazines. There are weight loss magazines and books of all shapes and sizes. Brochures about what to do if your ten-year-old regularly bedwets, and ads about how you should consult your doctor to see if (insert over-the-counter-drug here) is right for you.. There are board books that have been ripped up by countless filthy fingers. Who knows what sort of bacteria is just breeding and flourishing between the cracks of those pages...

The toys, however, are the worst of all. In the doctors' offices, there are those wire things with about a dozen wooden bead that you move around on the wires. I get the vibe that they are supposed to be fun. However, when I tried to move them around, the fun factor could pretty much be described on the same level as watching grass grow. There are also grubby monster trucks with wheels that don't work. Most of the toys are pretty much useless.
